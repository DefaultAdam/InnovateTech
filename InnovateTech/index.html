EU regulation forces Apple to change

×

A European Union law requires phone manufacturers to adopt a common charging connection by December 2024 to save consumers money and cut waste.

‎

The European Union (EU) passed a law requiring all electronic devices sold in the EU to use a common charging port. This law, known as the "Common Charger Regulation", was adopted in September 2021 and will come into force in December 2024.

‎

The EU's decision to require a common charging port for electronic devices was motivated by a number of factors, including:

1- To reduce electronic waste. The EU estimates that the current situation, where there are multiple different charging ports in use, leads to the unnecessary disposal of millions of chargers every year.

2- To make it easier for consumers. Under the current system, consumers often have to buy a new charger every time they buy a new device. This can be inconvenient and expensive.

3- To promote innovation. The EU believes that a common charging port will encourage innovation in the development of new electronic devices.

‎

Furthermore, Apple is planning to let users install alternative app stores on iOS.

The plans are reportedly being spurred on by the EU’s Digital Markets Act (DMA), which is meant to enact “rules for digital gatekeepers to ensure open markets” when its restrictions become a requirement in 2024

How does ChatGPT work?

×

GPT in ChatGPT stands for Generative Pre-Trained Transformers

‎

Pretrained artificial intelligence has been tested on a large dataset to solve problems.

‎

ChatGPT provides a response based on the context and intent behind a user's question.

‎

AIs pre-train using two principle approaches: Supervised and non-supervised.

Supervised pre-training is a process where a model is trained on a labeled dataset, where each input is associated with a corresponding output.

Non-supervised pre-training is the process by which a model is trained on data where no specific output is associated with each input. Instead, the model is trained to learn the underlying structure and patterns in the input data without any specific task in mind.

‎

The dataset used to train ChatGPT is huge. ChatGPT is based on the GPT-3, a library of over 45 terabytes of text data.

‎

This massive amount of data allowed ChatGPT to learn patterns and relationships between words and phrases in natural language at an unprecedented scale, which is one of the reasons why it is so effective at generating coherent and contextually relevant responses to user queries.

The US has lifted the ban on Apple smart watches temporarily.

×

‎

However, after the appeals court paused a previous ban on imports, Apple has won the right to import two of its smartwatches into the United States.

‎

Apple was ordered in October 2023 to stop importing and selling Series 9 and Ultra 2 watches, following a complaint from medical technology company ‘Masimo’ that Apple had unlawfully used proprietary software patents. Masimo accused Apple of stealing its pulse oximetry technology and incorporating it into Apple watches.

‎

Apple had appealed the ban that was imposed by the US International Trade Commission (ITC). But asked the US Court of Appeals to grant a pause on it in the meantime.

‎

Apple said it would suffer "irreparable harm" if the ban remained for the two weeks it thought it would take to get a response to its appeal from the US ITC.

‎

It requested to pause the ban at least until US Customs decides whether the redesigned versions of its watches infringe Masimo's patents.

The customs office is to make its decision on 12 January.

New York Times sues Microsoft and OpenAI

×

The New York Times sued OpenAI and Microsoft for copyright infringement

‎

The lawsuit, filed in Federal District Court in Manhattan, contends that millions of articles published by The Times were used to train automated chatbots that now compete with the news outlet as a source of reliable information.

‎

The suit does not include an exact monetary demand. But it says the defendants should be held responsible for “billions of dollars in statutory and actual damages” related to the “unlawful copying and use of The Times’s uniquely valuable works.”

‎

At the same time, OpenAI and other A.I. tech firms — which use a wide variety of online texts, from newspaper articles to poems to screenplays, to train chatbots — are attracting billions of dollars in funding.

‎

Microsoft has previously acknowledged potential copyright concerns over its A.I. products.

‎

None of these lawsuits have yet been resolved.

Sam Bankman-Fried FTX crypto-fraud case

×

In November 2022, FTX's collapsed in 10 days, starting on November 2 and ending on November 12. CoinDesk leaked an article containing FTX balance sheet. Binance initially announced it would sell all its FTT tokens because of the mishandled and blurred funds.

‎

What is FTT? Cryptocurrency platforms often create their own tokens to attract new customers. As more people invest and demand a digital token, its value increases, so platforms may offer perks. FTX created its own digital token called FTT which offered discounts and rewards.

‎

In early 2022, the price of Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies started to decline drastically from its high in the beginning of 2021. Many major platforms started shutting down, except for FTX, which continued acquiring competitors.

‎

FTX and its sister companies did not produce balance sheets showing assets and liabilities, which is standard financial reporting procedures. FTX's balance sheets were never audited because it was a private company. Without these audits, there was no record of cash flow or assets to show the company could cover liabilities or customer assets. FTX balance sheets showed assets were less than Bankman-Fried had stated.

‎

Authorities arrested Bankman-Fried on December 12, 2022, for multiple fraud charges with FTX. Bankman-Fried was indicted by the U.S. District Court on eight criminal charges, including money laundering, wire fraud, campaign finance violations and securities fraud.

‎

Bankman-Fried was released from custody with a $250 million bond, which is the largest in history.

The danger of your digital footprint

×

What is a digital footprint?

‎

Digital footprint or digital shadow refers to one's unique set of traceable digital activities, actions, contributions, and communications manifested on the Internet or digital devices.

‎

You have a digital footprint if you own a smartphone, have an email address or social media profile, or have ever made an online purchase.

‎

A digital footprint can have several positive outcomes, such as creating a strong online presence and enhancing personal branding.

‎

Despite its benefits, an unmanaged or poorly managed digital footprint can pose significant risks. Online activity can be monitored by potential employers, educational institutions, and even hackers.

‎

In essence, your digital footprint can act as a virtual paper trail, leading directly back to you. This can expose you to many privacy risks, including cyberstalking, harassment, and even physical threats. Therefore, internet users must remain vigilant and take proactive measures to protect their online privacy.
